## Assignment 1.1: Data Story Critique
<a href="https://www.propublica.org/article/averhealth-drug-testing-child-welfare-michigan"> A Child Welfare Agency Doubted the Accuracy of Drug Tests Used in Court. The Testing Company Dodged Questions. </a>

In this recent investigation by ProPublica, Averhealth, a major drug testing company used by state child welfare agencies, was found to have produced unreliable results due to poor laboratory practices and regulations. The company also ignored warnings from internal staff and accredited bodies while under contract with the state of Michigan. The reporters used a variety of methods to gather their data, including interviews with former Averhealth employees, officials from Michigan’s child welfare agency, company representatives, people in family court and anyone else who could provide more information on Averhealth. Reporters also used court documents, lawsuits, public records, and accreditation findings to uncover the issues with how Averhealth was operating.  
<p style="text-indent: 40px;"> 
  When the data came from an online record or source, the reporters directly linked it to show where their data was coming from. They also linked any relevant public statements from agencies announcing its removal of Averhealth from their drug testing processes. Although the investigation relied heavily on the data the reporters collected themselves, it lacked data visualizations to break up the text. When they could, the reporters included images from actual Averhealth labs, but nothing related to the data they found. The reporters could have done more to not only showcase the data but also make it very clear to readers how they collected it. The Washington Post, for example, always has sections under its graphs and data visualizations explaining its process. 
<p style="text-indent: 40px;"> 
  The ProPublica reporters did a good job of breaking up their numbers with anecdotes from families impacted by Averhealth's inaccurate testing, which helped with the overall flow of the story while also humanizing the numbers. Beyond this coverage, I would pursue a follow-up story to see if Averhealth has changed its methods or done anything in response to this investigation. Similarly, I would see if other agencies have stopped working with Averhealth or if any other families realized they were impacted by Averhealth's inaccurate testing. Also, I wonder if other organizations are engaging in the same practices as Averhealth. Overall, this investigation was well-reported and demonstrates how to blend data and anecdotes to produce an impactful story.
