# Data Story 2026.03.17
## <a href="https://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/pacific-northwest-saw-surge-in-ice-arrests-at-end-of-2025-data-show/">Pacific Northwest saw surge in ICE arrests at end of 2025, data show</a>  
### Date: March 11, 2026
### Publication: The Seattle Times 
This article does a good job of breaking down a large data set to determine what is actually going on. The reporters began by highlighting which Northwest counties saw the most ICE arrests in the last year, later focusing mainly on Oregon and Washington, and then specifically Yakima County. By dividing the data "per capita," the reporters show that Yakima saw the most ICE arrests with 185 arrests per 100,000 people. The arrests in this area are comparable to Portland and Minneapolis, but did not receive as much media attention, the reporters said. At this point, I expected to hear from families in Yakima County feeling the weight of these arrests. Instead, the reporters turned to Oregon's data and the "Operation Black Rose" behind the surge there. Although this is a data-heavy story and the various graphs do a good job of showing all the data, the story was lacking the human-impact perspective. It was dominated by experts, which is ok, but a future article should explore who is being directly impacted by this surge instead of letting experts speak for them.
