## HED: MPD practices heavy policing of Black and Brown neighborhoods 
<img width="620" height="296" alt="Screenshot 2026-02-26 at 11 53 00 AM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f743b4a3-4c39-41e2-b9f9-360892af2ef9" />

This graph shows, by ward, where external use-of-force was reported to have been used by officers from 2024 to 2025. The graph shows there were more use-of-force cases in Wards 3 and 7, which are predominantly Black and Brown communities. The wards with the fewest reports were Wards 1 and 2, with Ward 1 having a generally diverse demographic and Ward 2 containing most of the city's major landmarks, such as the National Mall. 

According to MPD's website, the office only started collecting and reporting use-of-force data in 2020 in response to local and national protests against excessive, racially biased use-of-force practices. This data is also corroborated by Open Data DC, a third-party platform managed by the District's Office of the Chief Technology Officer. The police department said the goal of publishing these annual findings was to increase transparency within the community. 

Although meant to ease the community, the heavy presence in Wards 3 and 7 and its broader implications remain a major concern for many Washingtonians. 

MPD defines use-of-force as "any physical coercion used to effect, influence, or persuade an individual to comply with an order from an officer." Officers must also prioritize de-escalation over resorting to forceful practices, yet the high number of police assigned to Wards 3 and 7 shows that more crime is expected to come from these areas. 

The department indicates force is used by officers who feel under threat, yet members of Wards 3 and 7 don't feel this is an accurate reflection of their neighborhoods.
